🧮 How It Works

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, considered the most accurate method by nutrition professionals. It calculates your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) and multiplies it by your activity factor to determine your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE).

📊 Understanding Your Results

Your maintenance calories are what you need to maintain your current weight. For weight loss, create a moderate deficit. For muscle gain, add calories gradually. Remember, consistency matters more than perfection.

⚡ Why Professional Guidance Matters

While this calculator provides a great starting point, everyone's metabolism is unique. Factors like muscle mass, genetics, hormones, and food quality all play crucial roles in your transformation journey.
